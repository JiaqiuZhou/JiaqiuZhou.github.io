How to be a Good writer

How to make Good write things!!!!

  • Ask Yourself:
    • Is this sentence easy to understand?
    • Is this sentence enjoyable and interesting to read?
  • Complex ideas don’t require complex language.
  • Scientific writing should be easy and even enjoyable to read!

Principles of effective writing

How to modify a paper?

  • Note the use of nouns instead of verbs
  • Note the use of vague words
  • Note the use of unnecessary jargon and acronyms
  • Note the passive voice
  • Note the distance between the subject and the main verb of this sentence

The Principles of writing

  • Cut unnecessary words and phrases;learn to part with your words;
  • Long words should be short; Repetitive words;
  • Be vigilant and ruthless
  • Use the active voice(subject+verb+object)
  • Write with verbs:use strong verbs,avoid turning verbs into nouns, and don’t bury the main verb!

How to cut the clutter

  • Avoid Dead weight words and phrases
    • As it is well known
    • As it has been shown
    • It can be regarded that
    • It should be emphasized that
  • Avoid Empty words and phrases
    • basic tenets of
    • methodologic
    • important
  • Long words or phrases that could be short
  • Unecessary jargon and acronyms
  • Repetitive words or phrases
    • Studies/examples
    • illustrate/demonstrate
    • Challenges/difficulties
    • Successful solutions

  • Adverbs

  • Very,really,quite,basically,generally,etc.

Cut the clutter, more tricks

  • Eliminate negatives

    • “she is not right” —> “she is wrong”
    • Not honest —> dishonest
    • Did not succeed —> failed

  • Eliminate superfluous uses of “there are/there is”

    • “There was a long line of bacteria on the plate”

      —>”Bacteria lined the plate”

  • Omit needless prepositions(消除不必要的介词)

Lecture 2 Latter two Writing principle

Use active voice

  • Active voice: “Subject verb object”(主语 动词 宾语)

What is the passive voice

  • Passive voice:“Object-Verb-Subject of just Object-Verb“
    • Classic example: “Mistakes were made”
  • Passive verb = a form of the verb “to be” + the past participle of the main verb
  • The main verb must be a transitive verb(that is, take an object)

How to convert passive voice

  • Find out the object, the verb and the subject in the sentence
  • Judge who actually use the verb
  • Judge “Who does what to whom?”

Use “We” and “I”

Why is it really ok to use “We” and “I”

  • The active voice is livelier and easier to read
  • You take responsibility for its content
  • Journals want this
  • Great authors use “we” and “I”

When is it OK to use the passive voice?

  • The methods section
    • What was done is more important than who did it!
    • Readers tend to skim the methods section for key words rather than reading it as prose
    • May be more effort than it’s worth to avoid using “we” and “I” in every sentence.

A few grammar tips

  • “Data are” not “Data is”
  • Affect vs.effect
    • Affect is the verb “to influence”
    • Effect is the noun form of this influence
  • Compared to vs. compared with
    • Compare to = to point out similarities between different things
    • Compare with = to point out differeces between similar things
  • That vs. which
    • “That” is the restrictive pronoun;cannot be eliminated
    • “Which” is the nonrestrictive pronoun;can be eliminated
  • Singular antecedents
    • Do not use “they” or “their” when the subject is singular.

小结

  • 尽量使用主动语句 we and I
  • 尽量使用动词，合适的动词，突出主要的动词

lecture 3: How to improve sentence structure

Experiment with punctuation

  • Vary sentence structure:Using a punctuation

  • Semicolon(分号):

    • Connects two independent clauses

  • Used to separate items in lists that contain internal punctuation

    • Example

  • Parentheses(括号)

    • Insert an aferthought or explanation(a word, phrase or sentence) into a passage

    • Example

  • Colon(冒号):

    • Use a colon after an independent clause to introduce a list, quote, explanation, conclusion, or amplification.

    • The rule of three‘s(lists, examples)

    • Use a colon to join two independent clauses if the second amplifies or extends the first.

    • Example:

  • Dash(破折号):

    • Use the dash to add emphasis or to insert an abrupt definition or description almost anywhere in the sentence.

Parallelism

  • Pairs of ideas joined by “and”, “or” , or “but” should be written in parallel form.Infinitive phrase and infinitive phrase

  • Lists of ideas(and numbered lists of ideas should be written)

  • Example

Paragraphs

  • Tips:

    • 1 paragraph = 1 idea
    • Give away the punch line early 及早给出观点
    • Paragraph flow is helped by:
      • logical flow of ideas
      • parallel sentence structures
      • if necessary, transition words(过渡词)
      • Make the last sentence memorable.Emphasis at the end!

  • Logical flow of ideas:

    • Sequential in time
    • General->specific(take-home message first!)
    • Logical arguments(if a then b;a therefore b:)
  • What’s the paragraph trying to convey?
    • Main idea of the paragraph
    • Supporting ideas->specifivs of how they differ
    • Sub-supporting ideas->definitions

More paragraph practice

A few more tips: repetition, key words, and acronyms

  • Repetition:
    • Is the second instance of the word even necessary
    • Is a synonym really better than just repeating the word
    • Needless synonyms:Banana->”the elongated yellow fruit”
  • Acronyms/Initialisms:
    • Use only standar acryonyms/Initialisms(e.g., RNA).
    • Redefine occasionally(reader don’t read start to finish)

  • 在一些有限的短语之间并行使用上“and”，“or” 等介词
  • 对于一个段落来说，主题观点要清晰，支撑观点的语句要突出
  • 段落最后一句要重复主题

Lecture 4: Practice all the skills

Overview of the Writing Process

Prewriting(70%)

  • Collect，synthesize and organize information
  • Brainstorm take-home messages(the main message)
  • Work out ideas away from the computer
  • Develop a road map/outline

Writing the first draft(10%)

  • Putting your facts and ideas together in organized prose

Revision(20%)

  • Read your work out loud
  • Get rid of clutter
  • Do a verb check
  • Get feed back from others

The Pre-writing Step

  • Get organized first
    • Don’t try to write and gather information simulataneously
    • Gather and organize information before writing the first draft
  • Organizing your thoughts
    • Spend more time organizing and less time writing.
  • Develop a road-map
    • Arrange key facts and citations from the literature into a crude road map/outline BEFORE writing the first draft
    • Think in paragraphs and sections..
  • Brainstorm away from the computer
    • Write on the go！
    • Work out take-home messages
    • Organize your paper
    • Write memorable lines
  • Compositional organization
    • Like ideas should be grouped
    • Like paragraphs should be grouped
    • Don’t “Bait-and-Switch” yips reader too many times

The Writing Step

  • Don‘t be a perfectionist!
  • The goal of the first draft is to get the ideas down in complete sentences in order
  • Focus on logical organization more than sentence-level details
  • Minimize the pain by writing the first draft quickly ande efficiently

Revision

  • Read your work out loud
    • The brain processes the spoken word differently than the writtern word
  • Do a verb check
    • Underline the main verb in each sentence
    • Find Lackluster verbs/passive verbs/buried verbs to modify
  • Cut clutter
    • Dead weight words and phrases
    • Empty words and phrases
    • Long words or phrases that could be short
    • Uncessary jargon and acronyms
    • Repetitive words or phrases/Adverbs
  • Do an organizational review
    • Tag each paragraph with aphrase or sentence that sums up the main point
    • Move paragraphs around to improve logical flow and bring similar ideas together
  • Get feed back from others
    • Ask someone outside your department to read your manuscript
    • Without any technical background, they should easily grasp:
      • the main findings/take-home messages
      • sinificance of your work
    • Ask them to point out particularly hard-to-read sentences and paragraphs!
  • Get editing help
    • Find a good editor to edit your work!

Checklist for the final draft

  • Check for consistency
    • Methods—>result
  • Check for numerical consistency
    • numbers in abstract match the tables/figures/text
    • numbers in the text match tables/figures
    • numbers in each table/figure match those in other tables/figures
  • Check your references
    • Do you have “references to nowhere”?
    • Reference does not provide the indicated information/fact

小结

  • 写作之前的准备，论文信息的收集完成后，论文事半功倍
  • 做好思路的整理，写草稿，做修改
  • 论文草稿完成后，利用checklist进行检查

Lecture 5: Manuscript

Tables and Figures

  • The foudation of your story
  • Editors,reviewers,and readers may look first at titles, abstracts,and tables and figures!

  • Figures and tables should stand alone and tell a complete story.The reader should not need to refer back to the main text.

  • Tips on Tables and Figures

    • Use the fewest figures and tables needed to tell the story
    • Do not present the same data in both a figure and a table

  • Tables vs. Figures

    • Figures
      • Visual impact/Show trends and patterns
      • Tell a quick story/Tell the whole story/Highlight a particular result
    • Tables
      • Give precise values
      • Display many values/variables

  • Table Title

    • Identify the specific topic or point of the table
    • Use the same key terms in the table title, the column headings, and the text of the paper
    • Keep it brief!

  • Table Footnotes

    • Use superscript symbols to identify footnotes, according to journal guidelines;
    • Use footnotes to explain statistically significant differences
    • Use footnotes to explain experimental details or abbreviations

  • Table Formats

    • Most journals use three horizontal lines: one above the column headings, one below the column headings, and one below the data
    • Follow journal guidelines RE
    • What not to do:
      • Remove grid lines!
      • Make sure everything lines up and looks professional!
      • Use a reasonable number of significant figures
      • Omit unnecessary columns

  • Types of Figures

    • Primary evidence
    • Graphs
    • Drawing and diagrams

Results

  • Results is not Raw data
    • Summarize what the data show
    • Avoid simply repeating the numbers
  • Tips for writing Results
    • Break into subsections, with headings
    • Complement the information that is already in tables and figures
    • Repeat/highlight only the most important numbers
    • Don’t forget to talk about negative and control results
    • Reserve the term “significant” for statistically significant
    • Reserve information about what you did for the methods section
    • Reserve comments on the meaning of your results for the discussion section
  • Use the active voice!
    • More lively!
    • Since you can talk about the subjects of your experiments, “we” can be used sparingly while maintaining the active voice!

Methods

  • Methods and Materials
    • Gice a clear overview of what was done
    • Give enough information to replicate the study
    • Be complete, but make life easy for your reader

Introduction

  • The introduction is easier to write than you may realize!
  • Follows a fairly standard format
  • Typically 3 paragraphs long

  • It is not an exhaustive review of your general topic

  • Corresponds to roughly 3 paragraphs

    • What’s Known(Paragraph 1)
    • What’s unknown(Paragraph 2)
    • Your burning question/hypothesis/aim(Paragraph 3)
    • Your experimental approach(Paragraph 3)
    • Why your experimental approach is new and different and important(fills in the gaps)(Paragraph 3)

  • Tips for writing an Introduction

    • Keep paragraphs short
    • Write for a general audience
    • Known->Unkown->Question/hypothesis
    • Emphasize how your study fills in the gaps
    • Explictly state your research question/aim/hypothesis
    • Do not answer the research question(no results or implications)
    • Summarize at a high level! Leave detailed descriptions,speculations, and criticisms of particular studies for the discussion.

Discussion

  • Showcase good writing
  • Start and end with the main finding
  • Don’t travel too far from your data
  • Focus on the limitations that matter,not generic limitations
  • Make sure your take-home message is clear and consistent

Abstract

  • Overview of the main story
  • Gives highlights from each section of the paper
  • Limited length(100-300 words, typically)
  • Stands on its own
  • Used, with title, for electronic search engines
  • Most often, the only part people read

小结

  • 图表需要清楚的描述本论文实验所说明的数据，按照要求格式编写
